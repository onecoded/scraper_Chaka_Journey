Welcome, Adventurer! You've been invited to experience seven days of breakthrough, connection, and play. This isn't about competition or challenges—it's about unlocking yourself through seven gates. Each gate represents a different aspect of being human, and behind each gate? A party that helps you become more YOU.

When you're ready to move deeper, you pass through a gate. Gatekeepers don't judge—they simply witness your readiness and welcome you to the next level of the party.
Your Journey: From survivor to lover to warrior to friend to speaker to mystic to yourself. Each gate opens new rooms, new games, new possibilities. No points, no scores, just experience and access.

THE CONSENT SYSTEM: Simple & Clear
GREEN (YES!) - I'm into this, approach me, let's play
 YELLOW (MAYBE) - This isn't fun right now, slow down, let's talk
 RED (STOP) - No, full stop, I'm out, respect my boundary
Everyone wears colored wristbands they can change anytime. Gatekeepers and hosts check in constantly. Yellow and Red are always respected, no questions asked.

THE CARPET CEREMONY FRAMEWORK
This is group process as transformation.
One person sits in the center (on the carpet). They answer a deep question. Then the community responds—not with advice, not with fixing, but with their own truth about what got activated in them.
HOW IT WORKS:
Person in Center shares their truth about a deep question.
Community responds by choosing one of 4-5 authentic responses:
RESONANCE - "I feel that too. When you said ___, I felt it in my body because I..."
TRIGGER - "That activated something in me. I felt [angry/scared/judgmental/defensive] because..."
MIRROR - "I see myself in you. The part of me that ___ recognizes your ___..."
SHADOW - "I judge you for that because I'm afraid of that same quality in myself..."
CELEBRATION - "I celebrate that in you because I wish I could ___..."
The person in center doesn't respond. They just receive the community's truth. This creates a web of authentic connection.

"Slow down. Look them in the eye. Answer fully."
IF THEY BREAK DOWN:
"Keep walking. You can cry and keep going. Don't stop."
IF THEY SHUT DOWN:
"Where did you go? Come back. We're right here. Try again."
IF THEY GO DEEP:
"Yes. Stay with that truth. Keep going. We've got you."

PHASE 2: STRENGTH GAUNTLET
Same walk, but now each person shouts your STRENGTHS:
"You are brave!"
"You are powerful!"
"You are worthy!"
FACILITATOR RESPONSES:
IF THEY DEFLECT:
"You just shook your head no. Walk it again. RECEIVE it this time."
IF THEY CRY:
"Yes. Let it land. You ARE those things. Keep walking."
IF THEY SMILE/LIGHT UP:
"Look at that shift! Your body knows it's true. How does that feel?"
IF THEY STAY DEFENDED:
"Your arms are crossed. Open up. Let them in. Try again."
IF THEY RECEIVE IT:
"Beautiful. You just let in the truth. That's warrior receiving love."

EVENING: BUCKETING CIRCLE (7 PM-12 AM)
THE SETUP: Small circles of 8-10. Each person gets 7-10 minutes.
PROMPTS (They choose one):
"When I betrayed myself..."
"What I'm most afraid to say is..."
"The boundary I haven't set is..."
"What I'm done tolerating is..."
THE RULES:
Speak without interruption
Group witnesses in silence
No fixing, no advice
When done: "Thank you" or "I hear you, brother/sister"
FACILITATOR INTERVENTIONS BASED ON WHAT EMERGES:
IF THEY INTELLECTUALIZE:
"You're analyzing. Drop into feeling. What's happening in your body right now?"
IF THEY RUSH:
"Slow down. You have time. Breathe. Start again."
IF THEY APOLOGIZE FOR TAKING TIME:
"You just apologized for existing. No. You get your time. Continue."
IF THEY GO NUMB:
"You went flat. I lost you. Where did you go?"
"Put your hand on your heart. Breathe. Try again from there."
IF THEY ACCESS REAL EMOTION:
"Yes. Stay there. We're with you. Keep going."
[After] "What just happened? What did you notice?"
IF THEY FINISH BUT SEEM INCOMPLETE:
"Is there more? Take your time."
IF THEY'RE TRULY COMPLETE:
"Thank you. That was real. How are you feeling now?"
[To group] "Anyone relate to what they shared? [hands up] You're not alone in this."

DAY 2: THE LOVER GATE - "I OPEN MY HEART"
MORNING GAME: SPEED INTIMACY (9-11 AM)
THE SETUP: Pairs rotate every 7 minutes. Mixed gender.
QUESTIONS (Get progressively deeper):
Round 1: "What's one thing that brings you joy?" Round 2: "When did you last cry?" Round 3: "What do you long for?" Round 4: "What are you most afraid of?" Round 5: "What do you need right now?"
FACILITATOR RESPONSES AFTER EACH ROUND:
AFTER ROUND 1 (JOY):
[To group] "Who found that easy? [hands] Who found that hard? [hands] Interesting..."
"Some of you looked happier talking about joy. Some looked sad. What's that about?"
AFTER ROUND 2 (CRYING):
"Who couldn't remember the last time? [hands] That's information."
"Who cried this week? [hands] Who cried during that question? [hands] Good."
AFTER ROUND 3 (LONGING):
"Who felt vulnerable sharing that? [hands] Welcome to Lover energy."
"Longing is beautiful. It means you're alive. Don't apologize for it."
AFTER ROUND 4 (FEAR):
"Who wanted to lie? [hands laugh] Who actually did? [hands] At least you're honest about lying."
"Fear shared is fear halved. Notice how you feel having said it."
AFTER ROUND 5 (NEED):
"Who asked for what they actually need? [few hands] Who asked for something safe? [many hands] We'll work on that."

THE GRIEF CEREMONY (11 AM-2 PM)
THE SETUP: One person sits in center. They share about a loss they've never fully grieved.
No time limit. Could be 5 minutes, could be 20.
FACILITATOR RESPONSES BASED ON WHAT EMERGES:
IF THEY TELL THE STORY WITHOUT FEELING:
"You're giving me the facts. I need the feelings. What hurts?"
"Pause. Close your eyes. Go back to that moment. What do you feel?"
IF THEY START TO CRY BUT STOP:
"Don't stop the tears. Let them come. We can hold this."
"Your body wants to cry. Let it. We're right here."
IF THEY APOLOGIZE FOR CRYING:
"No apologies for tears. Grief is sacred here. Continue."
IF THEY SHUT DOWN:
"You went somewhere else. Come back. Stay with us."
"Put your feet on the floor. Feel the ground. Come back to the grief."
IF THEY RAGE:
"Yes! That's the anger under the sadness. Let it out!"
"Louder. LOUDER. We can handle it."
IF THEY SIT IN SILENCE:
"Take your time. Silence is okay. We're here."
[After 2-3 min] "What's happening right now?"
IF THEY COMPLETE:
"Thank you for letting us hold that with you. How are you feeling?"
"Look around. See these faces? You're not alone in your grief."
[To group] "Who else has ungrieved loss? [everyone's hand goes up slowly] See? We all do."

AFTERNOON GAME: THE APPRECIATION AMBUSH (2-4 PM)
THE SETUP: One person stands in center. Circle has 2 minutes to bombard them with genuine appreciation.
Fast. Overlapping. Loud. Specific.
"I appreciate how you..." "I'm grateful for..." "I see your..."
FACILITATOR RESPONSES BASED ON HOW THEY RECEIVE:
IF THEY DEFLECT ("Oh, it's nothing"):
"Stop. Do NOT minimize. Just say 'thank you.' We're going again."
[Circle repeats until they receive it]
IF THEY LOOK DOWN:
"Eyes up. Look at us. We mean this. Receive it."
IF THEY CRY:
"Those are good tears. Keep receiving. You deserve this."
IF THEY SMILE/OPEN:
"Yes! Look at your face! You're letting it in. Keep going, circle!"
IF THEY FREEZE:
"You froze. Breathe. You're safe. This is love. Can you let it in?"
AFTER THEIR 2 MINUTES:
"How does your body feel right now compared to 2 minutes ago?"
"What was harder - giving or receiving appreciation?"
"What did you notice about wanting to deflect?"

EVENING: THE LOVE CONFESSIONS (7 PM-1 AM)
THE GAME: Write a love letter to someone (present or absent) you've never told.
Read it aloud to the group as if they're there.
FACILITATOR RESPONSES BASED ON WHAT HAPPENS:
IF THEY READ IT LIKE A BOOK REPORT:
"Stop. This is a love letter. Feel it. Read it like you mean it."
IF THEY BREAK DOWN:
"Keep reading. You can cry and read. Don't stop."
IF THEY RUSH THROUGH:
"Slow down. Every word matters. Start that paragraph again."
IF IT'S TO SOMEONE WHO DIED:
"They can hear you. Say it all. Leave nothing unsaid."
[After] "What do you think they'd say back?"
IF IT'S TO SOMEONE STILL ALIVE:
"Are you going to actually send this or give it to them?"
[If no] "What stops you? What are you afraid of?"
[If yes] "When? Be specific. Let this group hold you accountable."
IF THEY ACCESS DEEP LOVE:
"Yes. That's your heart fully open. Stay there."
[After] "How does it feel to let yourself love that fully?"
AFTER EACH LETTER:
[To group] "Who relates? Who has unsaid love? [hands] We're going to change that tonight."

DAY 3: THE MAGICIAN GATE - "I SEE THE SHADOW"
MORNING GAME: THE JUDGMENT JAR (9-11 AM)
THE SETUP: Jar filled with judgment cards.
Pull a card: "People who are weak." "People who are arrogant." "People who are needy." etc.
If you judge that quality, step forward and complete:
"I judge people who are ___ because..."
"The truth is, I am also ___ when I..."
"What I'm protecting by judging is..."
FACILITATOR RESPONSES BASED ON THEIR PROCESS:
IF THEY DENY HAVING THE QUALITY:
"Really? No one here believes that. Try again. Where are you like this?"
[To group] "Anyone ever seen them be ___? [hands go up, laughter]"
IF THEY MINIMIZE IT:
"You said 'a little bit.' How about 'a lot'? Try that on."
IF THEY JUSTIFY THE JUDGMENT:
"You're explaining why your judgment is valid. That's not the work. Own that YOU are also this."
IF THEY INTELLECTUALIZE:
"Stop analyzing. Feel it. Where in your body do you judge this?"
IF THEY ACTUALLY OWN IT:
"Yes! That's integration. You just reclaimed your shadow. How does that feel?"
[To group] "This is the work. We judge in others what we can't accept in ourselves."
IF THEY HAVE AN INSIGHT:
"What just landed? Say more about that."
"What changes when you own this part of yourself?"

THE MIRROR PROCESS (11 AM-1 PM)
THE SETUP: Dyads. Partners take turns:
Person A: "Something about you that triggers me is..." Person B: (Receives in silence) Person A: "The truth is, what I see in you is actually MY..." Person B: "Thank you for showing me that mirror."
Switch.
FACILITATOR INTERVENTIONS BASED ON WHAT EMERGES:
IF THEY MAKE IT ABOUT THE OTHER PERSON:
"Stop. This isn't about them. This is about YOU. What's YOUR trigger?"
IF THEY'RE TOO NICE:
"You're being polite. Get real. What actually bugs you about them?"
IF THEY GET DEFENSIVE:
"Your partner just gave you a gift - they showed you your shadow. Can you receive it?"
IF THEY ATTACK:
"Whoa. Too far. This isn't a roast. Name the trigger, own your part, move on."
IF THEY GO DEEP:
"Yes. That's the real work. You just saw yourself in the mirror."
AFTER DYAD WORK - FULL GROUP:
"Who discovered something about themselves? [hands] What did you see?"
"Who got defensive? [hands] What were you protecting?"
"Who's grateful for the mirror? [hands] That's maturity."

AFTERNOON GAME: SHADOW CHARADES (2-4 PM)
THE SETUP: Act out your disowned shadow quality.
Are you the "nice person" who denies their aggression? Act AGGRESSIVE. Are you the "strong one" who denies neediness? Act NEEDY. Are you the "smart one" who denies confusion? Act CONFUSED.
Exaggerate it. Have fun with it.
FACILITATOR RESPONSES:
IF THEY HOLD BACK:
"Bigger! You're still being too controlled. Let it rip!"
IF THEY GO FULL OUT:
"YES! That's your shadow! Look at you owning it!"
IF IT'S FUNNY:
"Laugh! Shadow work doesn't have to be heavy. This part of you is hilarious!"
IF IT FEELS SCARY:
"I know this feels dangerous. It's not. You're safe. Keep going."
AFTER EACH PERSON:
"How did that feel to embody that quality?"
"What's the gift in this shadow part?"
"Can you integrate it? 'I am also ___'? Say it."

EVENING: THE CONFESSION BOOTH (7 PM-2 AM)
THE SETUP: One person in center. They confess something they've never forgiven themselves for.
Full truth. No justifying.
FACILITATOR RESPONSES BASED ON WHAT THEY SHARE:
IF THEY JUSTIFY/EXPLAIN:
"Stop explaining. Just tell us what you did. The truth."
IF THEY MINIMIZE:
"You said 'it wasn't that bad.' Was it bad? Say it like it was bad."
IF THEY CAN'T SAY IT:
"What's the worst part? Say that part first. Get it out."
IF THEY CONFESS FULLY:
"Thank you for that truth. That took courage."
"How does it feel to say it out loud finally?"
IF THEY'RE DROWNING IN SHAME:
"Look around this circle. Do you see judgment? [heads shaking no]"
"You are not your worst moment. Say it back to me."
IF THEY WANT FORGIVENESS:
"Can you forgive yourself? Not someday - right now?"
"What would it take to forgive yourself?"
IF THEY CAN'T FORGIVE THEMSELVES:
"What's the payoff for holding onto this? What does your shame protect you from?"
AFTER CONFESSION:
[To group] "Who else carries unforgivable shame? [hands] Stand up. All of you."
[To person in center] "Look. You're not alone. None of us are."

DAY 4: THE SOVEREIGN GATE - "I BLESS AND SERVE"
MORNING: THE INTEGRATION GAME (9-11 AM)
THE SETUP: Musical chairs with archetypal stations.
When music stops, go to whatever station you land on:
WARRIOR: Stand strong, declare a boundary
LOVER: Put hand on heart, say what you love
MAGICIAN: Name your shadow, integrate it
SOVEREIGN: Bless someone, declare your purpose
Music plays. Rotate through all four multiple times.
FACILITATOR RESPONSES:
IF THEY KEEP CHOOSING SAME ARCHETYPE:
"You keep going to Warrior/Lover/etc. Try the one you avoid. Go!"
IF THEY'RE IN THEIR HEAD:
"Stop thinking. Feel into the archetype. Embody it. Try again."
IF THEY LIGHT UP AT ONE:
"You came alive at Lover/Warrior/etc. What's that about?"
IF THEY STRUGGLE WITH ONE:
"You're uncomfortable being Sovereign/Magician/etc. Stay there. Don't leave yet."
AFTER THE GAME:
"Which archetype felt most natural?"
"Which one felt scariest?"
"Can you access all four? Show me - right now, cycle through them."

THE THRONE WORK (11 AM-2 PM)
THE SETUP: Literal throne in center. One person sits.

"I am Warrior when I..."
"I am Lover when I..."
"I am Magician when I..."
"I am Sovereign when I..."
"I am here to serve..."
FACILITATOR RESPONSES BASED ON THEIR ENERGY:
IF THEY'RE TENTATIVE:
"Say it bigger. You're sitting on a throne. Own it. Again!"
IF THEY'RE PERFORMATIVE:
"You're performing. Drop the show. Say it real. Try again."
IF THEY CRY:
"Yes. That's the truth landing. Keep going."
IF THEY CAN'T NAME THEIR PURPOSE:
"Don't know? Guess. What feels true right now? Say that."
IF THEY NAIL IT:
"Yes! That's sovereignty. Look at you. How does that feel?"
[To group] "Did you all feel that shift? That's what integration looks like."
IF THEY DOWNPLAY THEIR PURPOSE:
"You said 'just' - 'I'm just here to...' Say it without 'just.' It's not small."
AFTER THEY SPEAK:
"Stand up. Bow to this community. They witnessed your wholeness."

AFTERNOON: THE PURPOSE ACCELERATOR (2-5 PM)
THE GAME: Speed dating for purpose accountability.
3 minutes per partner. Complete these:
"My purpose is..."
"My first step is..."
"I'll do it by [date]..."
"Will you hold me accountable?"
Partner says: "Yes, I'll check in. I believe in you."
Rotate. Do this 10 times with 10 different people.
FACILITATOR INTERVENTIONS:
IF PURPOSE IS VAGUE:
"Too general. Be specific. What EXACTLY are you building/creating/serving?"
IF NO TIMELINE:
"When? Give me a date. Not 'someday.' WHEN?"
IF THEY HEDGE:
"You said 'I'll try to...' No. 'I will...' Say it again."
IF THEY GO BIG:
"Yes! That's a real purpose. How does saying that out loud feel?"
IF THEY GO SMALL:
"Is that really it? Or are you playing safe? What's the REAL purpose?"
AFTER THE EXERCISE:
[To group] "Who has 10 people holding them accountable now? [everyone]"
"Who's going to actually do the thing? [hands] Good. We'll check."

THE SETUP: One at a time, each person sits in center.
Community speaks blessings:
"I bless your courage..."
"I bless your heart..."
"I bless your shadow..."
"I bless your purpose..."
Person receives in silence.

AFTER ALL BLESSINGS:

"Stand up. Bow to them. Bow to yourself."
